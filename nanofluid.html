Electrochemically Exfoliated Graphite Nanofluids

+ Side Project – Transient-Hot-Wire Instrument Design

 Project Synopsis

Our initial ambition was to synthesize single-layer graphene for heat-transfer fluids, but the scope expanded: we also needed an in-house instrument to measure thermal conductivity. To stay on schedule we pivoted to a greener, scalable route—electrochemical exfoliation of graphite (EEG)—and split the work into two tractable streams:

Stream I – EEG Nanofluid Formulation

  • Compared two exfoliation routes:
    • 0.5 M H₂SO₄ only (EEG-1)
    • 10 min pre-intercalation in 1 M NaOH + H₂SO₄ (EEG-2)
  • Characterised flakes via SEM, FTIR, UV-Vis, and XRD.
    → Multilayer graphite (≈ 8–12 nm stacks) with 12–17 at % oxygen.
  • Dispersed 0.05–0.5 wt % EEG in DI water → visually stable for >3 months.
  • Thermal-conductivity gain: up to 25 % at 0.5 wt % (EEG-2).

Stream II – THW Instrument Design

  • Built a constant-current Transient Hot Wire (THW) rig around a 50 µm platinum wire (14.5 cm).
  • Used a Gamry Interface 1010E potentiostat for current control & DAQ.
  • Automated data reduction in Python (slope of ΔT vs ln t).
  • Calibration with DI water returned 0.578 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹ @ 25 °C (literature 0.58) → ±1.5 % accuracy.

 Key Results

  • EEG-2 nanofluid (NaOH + H₂SO₄) delivered 17–25 % TC enhancement over water; EEG-1 peaked at 16 %.
  • NaOH pre-treatment reduced d(002) spacing from 0.337 nm to 0.333 nm and thinned stacks to ≈ 27 % fewer layers, boosting phonon transport.
  • No harsh oxidants (KMnO₄/Hummers) → low-hazard, low-cost process.

 What I Learned

  • Tactical scope-shifts can rescue a project without diluting its impact.
  • Instrumentation design and materials chemistry are mutually reinforcing—owning the measurement tool increased data trust.
  • Green electro-exfoliation is a viable pathway to scalable heat-transfer fluids.
