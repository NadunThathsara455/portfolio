Michelin R&D Internship (Dec 2023 – Jun 2024)

Applied polymer science, compound mixing, and advanced DMA to optimize solid-tire performance

 Core Projects

1 · Frequency-Dependent DMA Analysis

  • Implemented frequency-sweep & WLF-mode protocols on DMA +100.
  • Built mastercurves to predict tan(δ), E′, E″ across 1–100 Hz.

2 · Mastication-Time Optimisation

  • Quantified impact of internal-mixer mastication (2–10 min) on Mooney, ODR, tensile and DMA metrics.
  • Identified optimum mastication time ranges for increased performance.

3 · Tan(δ) (Tension-Mode) vs Rolling Resistance

  • Correlated lab-scale tension-mode tan(δ) with full-scale drum tests.
  • Enabled early screening of sample compounds, reducing rubber compound waste.

 Hands-On Experience

  • Compound Mixing: Banbury & internal mixers, two-stage and re-mastication; pigment, filler & curative addition.
  • Dynamic Mechanical Analysis (DMA): tension, compression & frequency modes; WLF shift.
  • Rubber Testing: ODR/MDR cure kinetics, Mooney viscosity, RPA, HBU blow-out, tensile & tear.
  • Data Processing: Python + Pandas workflow for curve fitting & mastercurve generation.
  • Lean & HSE systems: TLBO daily management, ARM risk assessment, LOTO compliance.

 Key Takeaways

  • Bridged lab visco-elastic metrics to tire-level rolling resistance, enhancing early-stage screening.
  • Learned full design–mix–test–iterate cycle in an ISO-9001 / ISO-17025 environment.
  • Developed confidence in scaling mixer recipes and troubleshooting compound composition issues.
